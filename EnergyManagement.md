Question 1: What are the activities you do that make you relax (Calm Quadrant)?

Answer:
a- Listening to soft music or lo-fi songs
b- Going for a peaceful evening walk
c- Reading books that are not related to work or studies
d- Doing deep breathing or meditation for a few minutes
e- Spending quality time with family or pets
f- Having a cup of tea and enjoying some quiet time

Question 2: When do you find yourself getting into the Stress Quadrant?

Answer:
a- Learning a new and difficult topic
b- Working under a tight deadline
c- Trying to fix a difficult bug or problem
d- Preparing for interviews or exams
e- Managing many tasks at the same time

I enter the stress quadrant whenever the work feels more challenging than the time, skills, or resources I currently have.

Question 3: How do you know that you are in the Excitement Quadrant?

Answer:
I know I am in the excitement quadrant when:

a- I feel energetic and motivated
b- I enjoy the work I am doing
c- I feel curious and eager to learn more
d- I feel proud after solving a difficult problem
e- I am excited to share my achievements with others

At that time, I feel positive, confident, and full of enthusiasm.

Question 4: Sleep Is Your Superpower – Key Points

Answer:

a. Lack of sleep reduces learning and memory power.
b. Deep sleep helps move memories into long-term storage.
c. Poor sleep increases the risk of health problems.
d. Sleep deprivation affects hormone levels and reproductive health.
e. Lost sleep cannot be fully recovered later.
f. Sleep is a basic biological need, not a luxury.
g. Sleeping and waking up at the same time every day improves sleep quality.

Question 5: What are some ideas that you can implement to sleep better?

Answer:
a. Follow a regular sleep schedule.
b. Keep your bedroom cool and comfortable.
c. Avoid using phones and laptops before bedtime.
d. Reduce caffeine intake in the evening.
e. Keep your room dark and quiet.
f. Develop a relaxing bedtime routine.
g. Avoid alcohol before sleeping.
h. Spend some time in natural sunlight during the day.
i. If you cannot sleep, do a calm activity and try again later.

Question 6: Brain-Changing Benefits of Exercise – Key Points

Answer:

a. Exercise improves mood, focus, and concentration.
b. It strengthens important parts of the brain related to memory and decision-making.
c. Regular exercise lowers the risk of memory loss and brain diseases.
d. The positive effects on attention can last for several hours after a workout.
e. Exercise helps create new brain cells and improves memory.
f. Doing aerobic exercise 3–4 times a week is highly beneficial.
g. The benefits of exercise grow over time and support long-term brain health.

Question 7: What are some steps you can take to exercise more?

Answer:

a. Start with a short daily walk.
b. Set a fixed time for exercise every day.
c. Choose activities that you enjoy.
d. Walk or cycle whenever possible.
e. Take short stretching or walking breaks during work.
f. Exercise with a friend or join a fitness group.
g. Track your progress using an app or notebook.
h. Keep your workout clothes ready in advance.
i. Reduce screen time and spend more time being active.
