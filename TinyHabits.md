2- How can you use B = MAP to make making new habits easier? What are M, A and P.

B = MAP is a simple formula by BJ Fogg. It says a habit only happens when three things are present at the same time:
M = Motivation → your reason or desire to do it.
A = Ability → how simple the habit is to do.
P = Prompt → something that reminds you to do it.
So if you want to build a new habit, keep it simple, have a reason behind it, and set a reminder. If any one of these is missing, the habit probably won't stick.

3- Why it is important to "Shine" or Celebrate after each successful completion of habit?

When you celebrate after doing a habit, even something small like saying "nice!" to yourself, your brain feels good. That good feeling makes your brain want to do it again. So celebrating helps turn an action into a habit because your mind starts linking that action with a good feeling.

5- What is the book's perspective about Identity?

The book says that if you want a habit to last, you need to change how you think about yourself. Don't just set goals like "I want to study more." Instead think "I am a person who studies regularly." When you start seeing yourself differently, the habits come naturally.

6- Write about the book's perspective on how to make a habit easier to do?

The book says you should make things simple. Remove anything that makes the habit harder. Prepare things in advance, start really small, attach the new habit to something you already do, and set up your 
surroundings so the habit feels easy and obvious.

7- Write about the book's perspective on how to make a habit harder to do?

The book says if you want to stop a bad habit, make it harder to do. Hide the triggers, make it inconvenient, make it less tempting, or put extra steps between you and the habit. The more effort it takes, the less you will do it.
